Key findings
Inequality

Hunts Point in the Bronx has 8× fewer trees per square mile than the Upper East Side — despite both being in the same city.

Income gap

Every single one of the 10 most underserved neighborhoods has a median household income under $35,000 per year.

Progress — but uneven

NYC added trees between 2005 and 2015, but the Bronx gained 42% while Queens actually lost trees — growth didn't go where it was needed most.

Heat & health

Neighborhoods with the fewest trees also score highest on urban heat vulnerability — meaning hotter summers on top of lower incomes and fewer resources.
