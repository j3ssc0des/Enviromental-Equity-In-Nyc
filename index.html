NYC Green Space Inequity

Street Tree Census Analysis — 2005 & 2015

View on GitHub
This project examines green space inequity across New York City's 90 Neighborhood Tabulation Areas by comparing tree canopy density with median household income from the 2005 and 2015 NYC Street Tree Census. Neighborhoods in the Bronx and parts of Brooklyn consistently show fewer trees per capita despite having lower incomes—a pattern that compounds environmental and public-health disparities. Data sources: NYC Street Tree Census 2015 NYC Street Tree Census 2005
