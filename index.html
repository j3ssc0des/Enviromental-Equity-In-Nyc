Key findings
Inequality

South Bronx neighborhoods like Hunts Point average just 600 trees per square mile, while Staten Island's suburbs top 1,290 — more than twice as dense, with incomes to match.

Income gap

Bronx neighborhoods with median incomes under $30,000 — Mott Haven, Hunts Point, Longwood — all rank in the bottom fifth of citywide tree density, while wealthier residential areas consistently lead.

Progress, but uneven

Every borough added trees from 2005 to 2015. Staten Island led at +25%, followed by the Bronx at +22% — yet Bronx communities that need greenery most remain near the bottom of city rankings.

Heat and health

Neighborhoods with the fewest trees also score highest on urban heat vulnerability, bringing hotter summers on top of lower incomes and fewer resources.
